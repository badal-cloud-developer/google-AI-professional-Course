# Google Prompting Essentials — Lab Practice

## Course: Create Custom Images with Gemini

**What this lab covers:**
Using Gemini to brainstorm, draft, generate, and edit custom images
for presentations, social media posts, posters, and other visual work.

---

## Step 1: Brainstorm Ideas for Your Image

**Goal:** Get Gemini to generate 3 creative visual concepts for your image.

**Prompt Template:**
```
I need some ideas for a [type of image, e.g. concert poster, social media 
post, or presentation slide] about [what the image is about, e.g. a music band].

The style should be [visual style, e.g. modern, retro, or minimalist] and 
the mood should be [mood, e.g. excited, calm, or mysterious].

Please give me 3 different creative concepts. For each concept, provide a:
- Description: A brief explanation of the visual layout.
- Color Palette: A set of colors that fit the mood.

OPTIONAL: I have attached an image for you to use as a reference. Please 
look at this image and use its [composition / color scheme / overall vibe] 
to inform your suggestions.
```

**What to look for in the output:**
- 3 distinct concepts with different visual directions
- A color palette for each concept
- Layout descriptions you can visualize

> 💡 **Tip:** Having trouble describing the style you want? Upload a reference
> image from your files or the web to show Gemini an example.

---

## Step 2: Draft Text for Your Image

**Goal:** Generate headline/copy options to go with your image.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Generate 3 options for text for a [type of project, e.g. poster, social 
media post, or presentation slide] about [the main subject or topic].

For context, [additional details or key information]. The tone of the 
writing should be [desired style, e.g. bold, professional, or playful] 
in order to [primary objective, e.g. get people to attend, explain a 
concept, or spark a conversation].

Please provide a few options ranging from short and punchy to more 
descriptive.
```

**What to look for in the output:**
- Options that range from short/punchy to more descriptive
- Tone that matches your goal
- Text you can directly use in Step 3

> 💡 **Tip:** The text you choose here will also give Gemini extra context
> when generating the image in Step 3.

---

## Step 3: Generate the Image

**Goal:** Combine your favorite concept from Step 1 and text from Step 2
to generate the actual image.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Based on the [chosen creative concept] and the text '[insert your favorite 
headline/text here]', generate a detailed image.

The image should feature [subject/main focus] in a [visual style, e.g. 
photographic, 3D render, or oil painting] style.

Ensure the composition includes [layout requirements, e.g. plenty of empty 
space at the top or a blurred background]. The overall mood should be 
[mood/vibe].
```

**What to look for in the output:**
- Does the image match the concept and mood you described?
- Is the composition suitable for adding text on top?
- Does it feel close to what you imagined?

---

## Step 4: Edit and Refine the Image

**Goal:** Tweak the generated image with follow-up prompts until perfect.

---

### 4a — Change the Composition
```
This is great, but can you move the [subject, e.g. the guitar] to the 
[position, e.g. left side] to leave more room for my text on the 
[opposite side, e.g. right]?
```

---

### 4b — Adjust Lighting / Color
```
Can you make the overall lighting [mood, e.g. brighter and more natural] 
and change the background colors to [specific colors, e.g. shades of blue 
and silver]?
```

---

### 4c — Add or Remove Detail
```
Please keep the same style, but add [detail, e.g. a subtle texture or more 
people in the background] and remove the [item to remove, e.g. the bright 
lens flare].
```

---

### 4d — Modify the Style
```
I like the layout, but can you change the style from [current style, e.g. 
a photo] to a [new style, e.g. a minimalist vector illustration]?
```

> 🔁 **Repeat Step 4 as many times as needed.** Each round of feedback gets
> you closer to the final image you want.

## Key Takeaways

- [ ] Being specific about style and mood gives better image results
- [ ] Combining visual concept + text in one prompt improves accuracy
- [ ] Iterating with small edits is more effective than rewriting from scratch
- [ ] Reference images help when you struggle to describe a style in words
